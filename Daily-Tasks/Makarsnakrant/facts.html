Makar Sankranti

Makar Sankranti is a Hindu festival celebrated in India and Nepal. It marks the transition of the Sun into the zodiac sign of Capricorn (Makara), signifying the end of the winter solstice and the start of longer days.

This festival is also celebrated as a harvest festival, marking the end of the winter harvest and the beginning of the new harvest season.

People celebrate by flying kites, taking holy dips in rivers, and preparing traditional sweets like tilgul (made from sesame seeds and jaggery).
